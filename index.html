ÁLVARO MARÍN

THIS IS NOT

A CURD

DRAWING / PAINTING

 

Pencil drawings. Multiple colors. Transparencies that allow the evolution of tones. Reds that suggest the yellow or blue reflection the green reflection; in the dark grey vibrates the warm terracotta and in other spaces the white on different tones nuances the form. In some drawings the folds of the paper transcend the square. One hundred and fifty pencils. All shades: yellow, green, blue, red, skin tones, gray, warm and cold, ready to apply freely. The painting is more rational, says Álvaro Marín, a painter with his own work and language.

The freedom in the drawing, the irregularity in the square, the spontaneity in the limits, the superimposition of squares, rectangles, lines that blend to give tone to the square that is not one, but a superposition of spaces; the perception of colors, some veiled, pure or mixed, others, propose another dimension of the work, wide and recognized of the artista.

The recent work that Álvaro Marín presents in the Maja Museum is, without a doubt, a work in motion: for the technique, the spontaneity, the possibility of discovery by the language that evolves, by the energy that reflects, because the time and space it suggests seems infinite. Because the painter, like art, does not stop, his state is of permanent movement.

Saúl Alvarez Lara
